### About Me

I work at the intersection of **natural language processing, machine learning,
document analysis, and digital humanities**.

My research focuses on building and evaluating models that remain useful when
the data is not clean, balanced, contemporary, or well-resourced. I am especially
interested in multilingual and historical documents affected by OCR, HTR, ASR,
domain shift, temporal variation, and incomplete supervision.

### Questions That Drive My Work

My research is guided by three connected questions:

> **How can NLP and language models accurately extract and reason over
> information when documents are noisy, multilingual, historical, or
> low-resource?**

> **How should we evaluate these systems so that metrics reflect semantic
> usefulness, robustness, calibration, and downstream impact—not only
> performance on clean benchmarks?**

> **How can models adapt across languages, time periods, domains, and document
> modalities while remaining grounded, interpretable, and reproducible?**

I explore these questions through dataset construction, controlled-noise
experiments, task-aware evaluation, error analysis, model adaptation, and
information extraction.

---

### 🔬 Research & Current Focus

I work on robust and reliable language and document AI, with a particular focus on:

- named entity recognition, entity linking, event extraction, and relation extraction;
- task-aware evaluation metrics that capture semantic usefulness and downstream impact;
- synthetic data, dataset construction, annotation, and benchmark design;
- LLM evaluation, fine-tuning, calibration, and confidence estimation;
- robustness under OCR, HTR, ASR, temporal variation, and domain shift;
- multilingual and low-resource NLP;
- historical and multimodal document analysis;
- interpretable connections between input noise, model representations, and predictions.
